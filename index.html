VII

Today I worked a bit on the "input" mini project. I counted at least 20 unique ways to input information, and I came up with dozens of different CAPTCHA tests. A CAPTCHA test is used to verify if the user is a human or not, and there are many reasons why this is important in security. For example, if you don't want bots bombarding your server with requests, then one of the easiest ways is to not allow them onto your servers. Secondly, if information is confidential, I am sure you wouldn't want unauthorized people to access it. The whole idea of finding unique, simple, and practical tests to trifle robots and allow humans to pass through is so fascinating to me, especially with the rise in technology and AI getting more and more capable. Without getting more sidetracked, I'd say my main takeaway is that I found that UX (User Experience) is what fascinates me and the stuff that really challenges me to learn more. This was also the day that I started to test different types of animations on different boxes and text models.
